REBECCA GOULD is a theatre director, producer and teacher. She is currently Creative Producer at Soho Theatre in London. In 2012 Rebecca produced ‘Hard Places’ for Tinderbox Alley, and the film and live broadcast of ‘I,Cinna (the poet)’ by Tim Crouch for the RSC and the World Shakespeare Festival. She then travelled to New York with Tim Crouch's King Lear, also teaching at Ohio State University on behalf of the RSC.

Rebecca was Associate Director at the Theatre Royal Plymouth, Young People's Projects Producer at the National Theatre, Director of the Education Company at the English Shakespeare Company, and Associate Director of Made in Wales. As a freelance director Rebecca has worked for the Sherman Theatre, Cardiff, the Royal Court (young writers programme), the National Theatre, Y-Touring, Bristol Old Vic, Chol Theatre and Sheffield Theatres.

As a Lead Practitioner in the RSC Education Department, she has led much of their CPD for teachers and co-authored the Methuen ‘Teachers Toolkit for Shakespeare’. Rebecca is also Learning Associate for the National Theatre and has recently devised work with young people to accompany the ‘One Man Two Guvnors’ tour. She previously directed for them ‘The Little Tempest’ and ‘The Wonderful Life and Remarkable Death of Renowned Magician Doctor Faustus’ in the Cottesloe Theatre.

Previously Rebecca produced ‘Not My Fault’, three new site-specific plays at the Eden Project for Theatrescience, the UK tour of ‘Speechless’ for Apples and Snakes and the British Council and the ‘Catalan-Wales’ new drama festival at Chapter, Cardiff.

Rebecca has considerable experience producing and managing international partnership work, particularly in India, where she has developed productive long-term relationships with venues and companies. She directed the Schools Young Writing Festival in Kolkata for 4 years for the British Council. Whilst at TRP, Rebecca staged two new adaptations of ‘Midsummer Night’s Dream’and ‘The Marriage of Anansewa’ with young actors from Plymouth and Sekondi Takoradi in Ghana. As co-director of Tinderbox Alley and co-founder of Theatrescience, she has produced several collaborative intercultural workshops, productions and events.
rebecca@tinderbox-consultants.com

JESSICA MORDSLEY is a project manager, evaluator, editor and writer. She was part of the consultancy team who developed the Creative Vision for the Dylan Thomas 100 celebrations.

She has evaluated a number of projects for companies including the British Council, the National Theatre and Soho Theatre, with a special interest in exploring the boundaries between the arts and sciences. She has also created education resources for the Royal Shakespeare Company.

As a project manager, Jess has managed literary events, tours and new plays. She co-produced the ‘Telling Stories’ international public engagement conference in India for the Wellcome Trust.

Jess has a PhD in Critical and Cultural Theory from Cardiff University, which looked at the boundaries between humans and other animals, and was previously a lecturer in Media Communication and Culture at the University of Gloucestershire. Her articles and reviews have appeared in a variety of publications including Hackney Citizen, Subject Matters and the Oxford Literary Review.

She regularly works for Parthian, an independent publishing company, for whom she recently edited ‘When I Came Home’ by George Brinley Evans and is now working on ‘Lucknow Ransom’ by Glen Peters.<
